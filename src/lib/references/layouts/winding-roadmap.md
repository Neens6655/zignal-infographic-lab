# winding-roadmap

A narrative journey that winds across the canvas, with illustrated milestone scenes along a central path. The path (river, road, trail) is the visual backbone — everything else is organized around it.

## Structure

### The Path
- A prominent S-curve or winding path that flows across the entire canvas — left to right, top to bottom, or a combination
- The path should be a RIVER, ROAD, or TRAIL that is contextually appropriate to the content
- The path occupies roughly 20-30% of the canvas width and weaves through the composition
- Start point in one corner, destination/conclusion in the opposite area
- The path connects all milestones in narrative order

### Milestone Scenes (5-8 milestones)
- Each milestone is a LABELED STOP along the path with its own illustrated vignette
- Milestones are numbered: "MILESTONE 1", "MILESTONE 2", etc. — placed in small flag, banner, or shield markers
- Each milestone has:
  - A **title** in bold (e.g., "THE DISCOVERY", "THE BREAKTHROUGH")
  - A **subtitle** with key details (dates, names, values)
  - A **text panel** with 2-4 lines of concise content
  - An **illustrated scene** — a small detailed drawing specific to that milestone's content (a building, a person, an instrument, a landscape, a diagram)
- Milestones alternate sides of the path — some on the left, some on the right
- Each milestone scene is roughly 15-20% of the total canvas area

### Central Focal Area
- The path should pass through or converge on one central focal area — the most important concept, turning point, or key insight
- This central area gets the largest illustration and most prominent text treatment
- It should be visually distinct — slightly larger, more detail, perhaps a different background treatment

### Scenery Along the Path
- The path doesn't exist in a void — fill the areas between milestones with contextual scenery
- Landscape elements: terrain, vegetation, water, sky, clouds, mountains, buildings in the distance
- Small secondary details: people walking, animals, tools, books, instruments — whatever fits the content
- These background elements create visual continuity and atmosphere

## Text Placement

### Title Block (Top)
- Main title: large, prominent, centered or left-aligned at the very top
- Subtitle: smaller, directly below, describing the narrative arc (e.g., "A hydrologist studying a river discovered why markets trend.")

### Milestone Text Panels
- Each milestone has its own contained text panel placed adjacent to its illustration
- Title in bold caps, subtitle/date below, then 2-4 content lines
- Text panels have subtle borders or background to separate them from the scenery
- Labels and annotations point to specific parts of the illustration

### Embedded Data
- If the content has formulas, charts, or statistics, embed them directly into the relevant milestone's illustration area
- Small hand-drawn charts, annotated equations, or data callouts within the scene
- Key numbers can be rendered large as standalone callouts

### Bottom Stats Bar
- Horizontal strip at the bottom of the entire infographic
- Contains 4-6 key statistics or summary metrics from the content
- Format: "METRIC LABEL | VALUE" separated by vertical dividers or pipes
- Slightly contrasting background (darker than the main canvas, or a different texture strip)
- This bar anchors the entire composition and gives the viewer a quick data summary

## Composition Flow

1. **Eye enters** at the title (top)
2. **Follows the path** from milestone 1 through each subsequent milestone
3. **Pauses at the central area** for the key insight
4. **Continues to the destination** (final milestone or conclusion)
5. **Lands on the stats bar** (bottom) for the data takeaway

## Best For

- Historical narratives and discoveries
- Scientific journeys and timelines
- Project roadmaps and strategy timelines
- Biographical stories
- Evolution of ideas or technologies
- Any content with a clear chronological or logical progression

## Recommended Pairings

- `aged-academia`: Historical/scientific narratives (BEST MATCH — produces museum-quality results)
- `storybook-watercolor`: Whimsical educational journeys
- `craft-handmade`: Friendly roadmaps and learning paths
- `technical-schematic`: Engineering and technical project paths
