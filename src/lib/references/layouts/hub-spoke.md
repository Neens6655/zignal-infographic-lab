# hub-spoke

A central concept radiates outward to connected ideas, components, or features. The hub is the visual anchor; spokes carry the supporting details.

## Structure

### Central Hub
- A large, prominent illustrated element at the center of the canvas
- Contains the core concept, main title, and a key visual metaphor or illustration
- Visually dominant — roughly 20-25% of canvas area
- Distinct visual treatment (different background, border, or emphasis)

### Spokes (5-8 spokes)
- Radiate outward from the hub in a roughly even distribution
- Each spoke terminates in a node containing:
  - **Illustrated scene**: A small vignette specific to that concept — an icon, diagram, scene, or chart
  - **Title**: Bold heading (max 4 words)
  - **Description**: 2-3 concise lines
  - **Labels**: Annotations on the illustration
- Spoke lines should be visually styled — not thin hairlines but styled connectors (ropes, wires, branches, beams)
- Optional: spoke lines can carry labels (relationship descriptors)

### Visual Density
- Fill the areas between spokes with background texture, secondary annotations, or decorative elements
- No large empty white areas — the entire canvas should feel populated
- Each spoke node gets its own mini-illustration, not just an icon

## Text Placement

- Main title: at the very top or integrated into the hub
- Hub label: the core concept name, large and central
- Spoke titles: bold, at each node
- Descriptions: concise text near each node
- Connection labels: on spoke lines if relationships matter
- Labels: directly on illustrations

### Bottom Stats Bar
- Horizontal strip at the bottom with 4-6 key metrics
- Format: "LABEL | VALUE" with dividers

## Composition Flow

1. Eye enters at the title/hub (center)
2. Radiates outward to explore each spoke
3. Connections between hub and spokes show relationships
4. Stats bar at bottom for summary data

## Best For

- Frameworks and ecosystems
- Product features around a core offering
- Central concept with components
- Team roles around a project
- Strategy pillars around a mission
