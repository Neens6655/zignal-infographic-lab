# bento-grid

A modular grid of illustrated panels, each telling one part of the story. Like a magazine spread or museum exhibit with distinct sections of varying sizes.

## Structure

### Grid Composition
- A grid of rectangular cells with MIXED sizes: hero cell (2x2), medium cells (2x1 or 1x2), and small cells (1x1)
- The hero cell (largest) contains the main concept — center or upper-left position
- No strict symmetry needed — asymmetric grids feel more dynamic and editorial
- 5-8 total cells, filling the entire canvas
- Subtle borders or gaps between cells (2-4px visual gap)

### Cell Content
Each cell is its own illustrated vignette, not just text on a background:
- **Hero cell**: Largest illustration with the primary concept, key visual metaphor, and main title treatment
- **Data cells**: Charts, statistics, key numbers rendered large with supporting illustrations
- **Scene cells**: Small illustrated scenes or diagrams specific to each topic
- **Quote/callout cells**: Featured quote or key insight with decorative treatment
- Every cell should have BOTH visual content AND text — never just text alone

### Visual Density
- Fill every cell completely — no empty white rectangles
- Each cell gets its own mini-illustration, icon scene, chart, or visual metaphor
- Background textures or colors can vary by cell to create visual variety
- Maintain style consistency across all cells

## Text Placement

- Main title: large, prominent, spanning the top or placed in the hero cell
- Cell titles: bold headings within each cell, clearly hierarchical
- Body text: 2-3 concise lines per cell maximum
- Statistics: rendered LARGE as focal points in data cells
- Labels: placed directly on illustrations

### Bottom Stats Bar
- Horizontal strip at the bottom spanning all columns
- 4-6 key metrics: "LABEL | VALUE" separated by dividers
- Slightly contrasting background

## Composition Flow

1. Eye enters at the hero cell (largest, most prominent)
2. Scans across the grid in reading order (left-to-right, top-to-bottom)
3. Each cell tells its own self-contained story
4. Stats bar at the bottom provides the data summary

## Best For

- Multi-topic overviews and executive summaries
- Feature highlights and product showcases
- Dashboard summaries and market analysis
- Mixed content types (data + narrative + concepts)

## Recommended Pairings

- `craft-handmade`: Friendly illustrated overviews
- `corporate-memphis`: Business summaries and reports
- `aged-academia`: Academic multi-topic surveys
- `bold-graphic`: High-impact feature showcases
