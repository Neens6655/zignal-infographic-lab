# structural-breakdown

A central subject deconstructed — exploded, cross-sectioned, or annotated — to reveal its inner workings. The subject IS the layout. Everything else orbits around it.

## Structure

### Central Subject (50-60% of canvas)
- One primary object, system, or subject rendered large and prominent in the center of the canvas
- Deconstructed using one of three methods:
  - **Exploded view**: Parts pulled apart along logical axes, floating with implied connections
  - **Cross-section / cutaway**: Sliced to reveal internal layers and hidden structure
  - **Annotated whole**: Intact but with callout lines identifying every key component
- Rendered in precise detail — isometric or 3/4 perspective preferred
- CRITICAL: Each component must be rendered in its OWN VIVID DISTINCT COLOR — like a color-coded anatomical diagram. The color-coding is what makes each part instantly identifiable and links it visually to its text description. Do NOT render all parts in the same neutral/realistic color.

### Callout Lines and Labels
- Thin precise hairlines connecting each part to its label
- Lines route cleanly — straight segments with right-angle bends, no spaghetti crossings
- Start with a small filled dot at the part, end at the text label
- EACH callout line uses the SAME COLOR as the component it references
- Section headers printed in that component's color — creating visual link between part and text
- Labels arranged in clean columns on the left and right sides of the illustration
- Part name in **bold** IN COMPONENT COLOR, brief description in regular weight gray below

### Narrative Text Zones
- **Left margin**: Contextual narrative — what the subject is, why it matters, background story
- **Right margin**: Technical details, specifications, or additional explanation
- **Bottom zone**: Process sequences, comparisons, or secondary diagrams
- Text blocks are editorial paragraphs (3-6 sentences), NOT bullet points

### Supporting Elements (optional)
- **Zoomed detail views**: Small enlarged views of specific parts in corners
- **Process sequences**: Step-by-step showing how something happens (e.g., landing sequence)
- **Scale reference**: Human figure, ruler, or "actual size" indicator
- **Secondary subjects**: If multiple objects, stack vertically in bands separated by rules

## Text Placement

- **Title**: Large, at the very top. Descriptive and engaging.
- **Subtitle**: 2-3 sentences setting the story context
- **Part labels**: Bold name + description, placed in margins connected by callout lines
- **Narrative blocks**: Editorial paragraphs in the margins providing story context
- **Byline / source**: Small text below title or at bottom

## Composition Flow

1. Eye enters at the title (top)
2. Drawn immediately to the central illustration (dominant visual)
3. Follows callout lines outward to read part labels
4. Reads narrative text in the margins for context
5. Explores detail views or secondary elements
6. Footer/sources at bottom

## Best For

- Product and device teardowns
- Anatomy and biology explanations
- Spacecraft, vehicle, and machine breakdowns
- Architectural explainers
- "How it works" stories
- System component analysis
- Any subject where understanding the PARTS explains the WHOLE
