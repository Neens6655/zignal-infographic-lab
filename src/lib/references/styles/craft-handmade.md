# craft-handmade (DEFAULT)

Warm, hand-drawn illustration with paper craft texture. Every element looks like it was drawn by a talented illustrator with markers and colored pencils on textured paper.

## Color Palette

- **Background**: Warm cream (#FFF8F0) or textured paper (#F5F0E6) with visible grain. Not flat white — show subtle paper texture.
- **Primary**: Warm saturated colors — coral (#E8735A), forest green (#4A7C59), warm blue (#5B8EBF), mustard (#D4A843)
- **Secondary**: Soft pastels for fills — peach, sage, powder blue, buttercream
- **Outlines**: Dark brown (#3D2B1F) or charcoal (#2C2C2C) for all drawn lines
- **Accents**: Bold highlights in red or orange for emphasis on key data

## Illustration Technique

### Hand-Drawn Quality
- Every element drawn with slightly imperfect, organic lines — as if with a felt-tip pen or marker
- Line weight varies naturally: thicker for outlines, thinner for details
- Fills use marker-style coloring: slightly uneven coverage, visible strokes
- Small imperfections are intentional — this is NOT vector art

### Illustrated Scenes
- Each section gets its own illustrated vignette with cartoon-style people, objects, and settings
- People: friendly proportions, simple but expressive faces, characteristic details (hats, tools, clothing)
- Objects: drawn with care and specificity — a laptop, a chart, a building, a tree
- Scenes: small illustrated settings that tell each section's story visually
- **Strictly hand-drawn — no realistic or photographic elements**

### Embedded Data
- Charts and graphs drawn by hand: slightly wobbly grid lines, hand-plotted data points
- Big numbers rendered large in a hand-lettered style
- Statistical callouts in hand-drawn speech bubbles or bordered panels
- Icons drawn as simple but specific illustrations, not generic shapes

## Style Enforcement

- All imagery must maintain cartoon/illustrated aesthetic throughout
- Replace any real photos or realistic figures with hand-drawn equivalents
- Maintain consistent line weight and illustration style across all sections
- Backgrounds should show paper texture, not flat digital color

## Typography

- Hand-drawn or casual font style for headings
- Clean, readable lettering for body text
- Keywords emphasized with LARGER, BOLDER hand-drawn treatment
- Labels placed directly on illustrations with leader lines if needed

## Layering & Depth

- Paper-cutout variant: drop shadows under panels suggesting layered paper
- Slight shadow under illustrations suggesting depth
- Background elements lighter/simpler than foreground
- Decorative doodles and small icons fill empty spaces

## Best For

Educational content, general explanations, friendly infographics, startup pitches, social media infographics, playful hierarchies, how-to guides
