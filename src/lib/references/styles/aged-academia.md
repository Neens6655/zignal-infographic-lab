# aged-academia

Museum-quality historical illustration on aged parchment. Think Da Vinci notebooks meets National Geographic maps meets Victorian scientific plates.

## Color Palette

- **Background**: Warm parchment (#F4E4BC → #E8D5A3) with subtle staining, foxing marks, and aged paper texture. Not flat — show gentle tonal variation across the surface.
- **Primary ink**: Deep sepia brown (#704214), iron gall ink black (#2C1810). All illustrations rendered as if drawn with real ink on real paper.
- **Earth tones**: Warm ochre (#C4943A), raw umber (#6B4423), burnt sienna (#8A4513), olive (#6B6B3E) for landscape and terrain elements.
- **Water/sky**: Muted cerulean (#5B8BA0), faded blue-green (#6B9B8A) for rivers, water, sky elements.
- **Accents**: Faded vermillion red (#B84233) for annotations, corrections, and emphasis marks. Muted gold (#B8943A) for highlights and important data.
- **Charts/data**: Use blue (#4A7B9B) and red (#B84233) for embedded chart elements. Keep data visualizations crisp against the aged background.

## Illustration Technique

### Hand-Drawn Quality
- Every visual element must look hand-drawn with ink — crosshatching for shadows, stippling for gradients, fine line work for detail
- Buildings, landscapes, people, and objects rendered with scientific illustration precision — accurate proportions but artistic style
- Slightly uneven line weight as if drawn with a dip pen — thicker on downstrokes, thinner on crossstrokes
- No digital-looking gradients or computer-generated effects

### Illustrated Scenes
- Each content section gets its own illustrated vignette — a small detailed scene that tells that section's story
- People shown in period-appropriate clothing with characteristic features (scholars, scientists, workers)
- Architecture and landscapes drawn with perspective and detail (buildings, pyramids, dams, bridges)
- Tools, instruments, and objects rendered with technical accuracy
- Natural elements (rivers, trees, mountains) drawn with naturalist attention

### Embedded Data Visualizations
- Mathematical formulas written in elegant hand-lettered notation on the parchment
- Charts and graphs rendered as if drawn by hand — slightly imperfect grid lines, hand-plotted data points
- Statistical callouts in rectangular bordered panels with aged paper fill
- Use small scatter plots, line charts, or bar charts embedded directly into the relevant sections
- Time series data shown as hand-drawn trend lines with annotation marks

### Portrait Treatment
- Key figures shown as detailed pen-and-ink portrait sketches (NOT photographs)
- Crosshatching for shading, fine detail for facial features
- Usually shown in 3/4 view or profile, within a subtle circular or rectangular vignette frame
- Include name and dates as hand-lettered labels below

## Text Panels

- Section titles in bold serif capitals, slightly larger, with subtle decorative flourish
- Body text in clean serif, arranged in neat text blocks with justified alignment
- Annotations and margin notes in slightly smaller italic or cursive hand
- Key terms in SMALL CAPS or **bold**
- All text must feel like it was typeset on the parchment, not floating above it

## Panel & Frame Treatment

- Content sections separated by subtle ruled lines or decorative borders
- Important callouts placed in bordered panels (double-line border, slightly darker parchment fill)
- Milestone labels use small flag or shield shapes with numbered indicators
- The entire composition should feel like one continuous illustrated manuscript page

## Texture & Aging

- Subtle paper grain texture throughout
- Slight yellowing and darkening at edges
- Occasional ink spots or foxing marks for authenticity (subtle, not distracting)
- Fold marks or slight creasing (very subtle) to suggest a real document

## Best For

Scientific narratives, historical discoveries, mathematical concepts, engineering stories, biographical timelines, academic explanations, any content that benefits from scholarly gravitas and visual storytelling depth.
