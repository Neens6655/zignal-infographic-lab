# executive-institutional

Clean, authoritative, light. McKinsey clarity meets Eleanor Lutz scientific precision. A one-page research brief that communicates depth through simplicity. Every element earns its place. White space is a feature, not waste.

## Design Philosophy

This infographic should feel like the best page from a JP Morgan annual outlook or a McKinsey Global Institute report — printed on quality paper. Light, airy, precise. The reader should think: "This was made by someone who deeply researched the topic and knows exactly what matters."

Every section answers: **"So what? What does this mean for the decision?"**

## Color Palette

- **Background**: Clean white (#FFFFFF) or the softest warm gray (#F8F9FA). The canvas is LIGHT. White space dominates. This is not a dark dashboard.
- **Primary text**: Charcoal (#1A1A2E) for headlines, dark slate (#334155) for body text. Strong contrast on white.
- **Navy accent**: Deep navy (#0F2B5B) used for the header bar, footer bar, section divider rules, and chart axis labels. Navy is a structural accent — not the background.
- **Data blue**: Institutional blue (#2563EB) as the primary data color in charts and callouts. This is the signature color.
- **Supporting data**: Muted teal (#0891B2), warm gray (#6B7280), soft coral (#E8735A for negative/risk). Maximum 3 data colors per chart.
- **Accent**: A single gold-bronze (#B8860B) hairline rule separating the header and footer from the content. Used nowhere else. Gold means "this is the frame."
- **Panel backgrounds**: Very light gray (#F1F5F9) for alternating panels or callout boxes. Barely visible — just enough to separate sections.

## Header Bar

A clean, authoritative horizontal strip at the top:
- **Background**: Deep navy (#0F2B5B) — the only dark element on the page
- **Title**: White, clean sans-serif, title case. Not all-caps unless short. Readable, not shouting.
- **Subtitle**: Light blue (#93C5FD) or white at 80% opacity — one sentence framing the "so what"
- **Right side**: Date or classification in small white text (e.g., "February 2026 | Global Research")
- **Bottom edge**: Thin gold (#B8860B) hairline rule, 1px

## Footer Bar

Matching horizontal strip at the bottom:
- **Background**: Same deep navy (#0F2B5B)
- **Left**: Source citations in small light text — "Sources: World Bank, UN Water, McKinsey Global Institute"
- **Center**: 4-6 key metrics in white, separated by thin vertical lines
- **Right**: Classification or branding
- **Top edge**: Thin gold hairline rule, 1px

## Typography

- **Title**: Clean premium sans-serif (Helvetica Neue, Inter, or similar aesthetic). Title case. 2-3x body size. Charcoal or white-on-navy in header.
- **Section headers**: Medium weight, ALL-CAPS, letter-spaced, charcoal (#1A1A2E). Small — not competing with the title.
- **Body text**: Regular weight sans-serif, dark slate (#334155), left-aligned. Clean, tight. 10-12pt equivalent.
- **Callout numbers**: The 2-3 most critical statistics rendered LARGE in institutional blue (#2563EB) or charcoal. These are the numbers the executive remembers.
- **Labels**: Small, precise, placed directly on or adjacent to data points. Never floating disconnected.
- **Language**: Precise, neutral. "Represents 70% of withdrawals" not "a massive 70%." No exclamation marks. No emoji.

## Illustrations

This is where Eleanor Lutz's influence shows. Illustrations are NOT banned — they are PRECISE, SPECIFIC, and CLEAN:

### Illustration Style
- Clean line illustrations in a single color (navy, charcoal, or institutional blue) on white background
- Thin, precise linework — think scientific diagram quality, not cartoon
- Specific to the content: a cross-section of pipe infrastructure, a schematic of irrigation systems, a simplified map showing water flow, a clean diagram of a desalination process
- No decorative flourishes, no drop shadows, no gradients within illustrations
- Illustrations should explain something — they are visual explanations, not decoration

### When to Illustrate
- Use a clean diagram when it explains a process better than text
- Use a simplified map when geography matters
- Use a schematic cross-section when showing how something works
- Use a clean icon only when it aids quick scanning (e.g., a water drop, a factory, a city outline)
- Do NOT illustrate for decoration. Every drawing must carry information.

### Illustration Treatment
- Line weight: consistent, thin (1-2px equivalent)
- Color: single-color (navy or charcoal) or two-color (navy + institutional blue)
- Background: transparent or white — illustrations sit on the white canvas
- Labels: clean, small text directly on the illustration with thin leader lines

## Data Visualization

### Charts
- Clean, minimal charts on white or very light gray background
- Thin axis lines in gray (#CBD5E1), subtle gridlines or no gridlines
- Data rendered in institutional blue (#2563EB) as primary, with muted teal and gray for secondary series
- Direct value labels on data points — no separate legends when possible
- Chart types: horizontal bar charts, line charts with subtle area fill, donut charts (thin ring, not full pie), clean scatter plots, slope charts
- No 3D. No gradients. No decorative chart elements.

### KPI Callouts
- Key numbers rendered LARGE in institutional blue or charcoal on white
- Below: small label in gray explaining the metric
- Below that: trend indicator (subtle arrow + percentage change)
- Arranged in a clean horizontal row of 3-4, evenly spaced

### Tables
- Clean tables with thin gray borders (#E2E8F0)
- Header row in light navy (#0F2B5B) with white text
- Alternating row shading in barely-visible gray (#F8FAFC)
- Right-align numbers, left-align text
- The key insight row highlighted with a thin blue left-border accent

## Composition

### Layout
- Strict 3-column or 4-column grid on white canvas
- Generous gutters (16-24px equivalent) between panels
- Generous margins — content does not touch edges
- Panels separated by subtle hairline rules or whitespace — not heavy borders

### Information Hierarchy (top to bottom)
1. **Navy header bar** with title + subtitle (8% of canvas)
2. **Hero insight panel**: One sentence + one large number + one clean visualization (15%)
3. **Content grid**: 3-4 analytical panels, each containing one chart or illustration + one insight + one "so what" (60%)
4. **Key metrics row**: 4-6 numbers in a clean horizontal strip (10%)
5. **Navy footer bar** with sources + summary metrics (7%)

### Whitespace
- Whitespace is INTENTIONAL and GENEROUS — this is what makes it feel premium
- Each section breathes. Nothing touches anything else.
- At least 10% of the total canvas should be pure white space (not including margins)
- Whitespace between sections is larger than whitespace within sections

## What This Is

- A research brief that happens to be visual
- Clean enough to print on white paper and hand to a board member
- Precise enough that every number has a source
- Clear enough that the "so what" is obvious in 5 seconds
- Beautiful in its restraint — the beauty comes from precision, not decoration

## What This Is NOT

- NOT dark or moody — the background is WHITE
- NOT dense or cluttered — whitespace is generous
- NOT decorated with textures, patterns, or ornamental elements
- NOT using more than 4 colors total in the content area
- NOT using cartoon, hand-drawn, or playful illustration styles

## Best For

Board presentations, investor updates, quarterly reviews, market analysis, strategy frameworks, due diligence summaries, economic research, competitive intelligence, risk reports, M&A analysis, portfolio reviews, annual outlooks
